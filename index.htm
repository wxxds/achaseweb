My Journey

Schooling

In December 2007 I started my first year of undergrad at The Art Institute of Washington. The first 2 years were graphic design focused and the last two years were spent in the inteactive media major. During these years I enriched my UI/UX design skills because I was still learning basic coding langauages like HMTL,CSS, JavaScript and PHP. My final 2 semesters were all portfolio classes which I averaged a 4.0 GPA. I graduated in September 2012, by October 2012 I gained employment with The Kennedy Center of Performing Arts as a Junior Developer.

Early career

My earlier dev days consisted of a combination of outdated cold-fusion code and table layouts which was The Kennedy Centers build. We used a ticket tracking system called ITSM for content updates and manual pushed and pull methods between servers. Halfway through this role, we were tasked to convert the entire website to use Boootstrap, Jquery and Jquery mobile. The brunt of the lower code environments, HTML and CSS were assigned to me, and most of the Javascript, Django and Terraform work was given to more seasoned devs. This created a opportunity for me to redesign most of the pages using layouts I thought fit best with the sites content.

In 2015 I moved on from The Kennedy Center, to small ticket sales company called Superstar Tickets. This role I choose not to add to my resume, as it is the only job I ever quit. The dev stack here was Material Lite Design and Django. I was the lead Frontend dev here, the only dev. I had some challenges with management as it was not a tech company and I was a young professional. Ultimately my designs and codes were implimented and kept for years after, until the company closed.

LCG Inc was my next stop. This is were my career as Lead dev really took shape.
